Hospitality

Here, patients are welcomed as guests.

The first days after surgery call for quiet, comfort and attentive people. Our hospitality exists for that — and for nothing else.

01

The idea

A hospital that learned from hotels.

Hospitals are usually places of hurry. Ours was designed for the opposite: the days of recovery deserve the rhythm, discretion and warmth of a fine stay.

Comfort doesn't replace clinical care — it is organized around it. Every detail exists so that rest comes effortlessly: the only task of the first days is to rest.

Hospitality

02

The room

Private, quiet, yours.

A door that closes, lighting that obeys, a bed made with care. No recovery here is shared.

  • Private room

    A space all your own for days that call for retreat — no shared spaces.

  • Hotel-grade linens

    Bed and bath linens chosen for the days when comfort stops being a detail.

  • True silence

    In a hospital with no emergency room, there are no urgent cases crossing the hallways: the ambient sound here is next to none.

The night

Here, silence is taken seriously.

Low lights, soft voices, gentle footsteps. Night was made for sleeping — even in a hospital.

03

Companion

Recovery is better with company.

Having someone you trust nearby changes the first days — the conversation that distracts, the glass of water within reach, the presence that soothes.

Our team will explain how companion stays work during your time at the hospital. Arrange the details at your consultation.

Team
04

Team

Attentive people, always close.

From anesthesia recovery to discharge, vital signs are monitored by the hospital's team — a team that lives and breathes plastic surgery every day.

Attention here isn't a memorized protocol: it's the closeness of people who know your case and are just steps from your room.

05

Assisted discharge

Discharge is a passage — not a goodbye.

You leave with clear post-operative instructions and follow-up appointments scheduled with your surgeon. Care continues at home — and returns to the hospital at every follow-up visit.

Admission, length of stay and discharge always follow individual medical judgment: comfort organizes the experience; the decision is clinical.
