02

Philosophy of practice

Safety and comfort are not details.

Dr. Rafael never opens a conversation with the procedure. He begins with the person: what bothers them, what they hope for, what their routine allows. Only then does the technique come in — chosen to serve the story of the person in the chair, and not the other way around.

“There is no transformation without learning and knowledge.”
Motto of the Anache Anbar brothers

In practice, the motto becomes method: understand deeply before recommending, explain with transparency before operating — including alternatives and limits — and treat the patient's safety and comfort as part of the surgical technique itself, not as an accessory to it.

04

A hospital of their own

A hospital designed for the after, too.

For someone who understands surgery as continuous care, the facility matters as much as the technique. Dr. Rafael follows his patients in a hospital of his own — surgical center, internal sterilization department, and private rooms with hotel-style amenities, in a flow 100% dedicated to planned elective surgeries.

No emergency room and no admissions for infectious diseases: the preparation, the surgery, and the first days of recovery all happen under the same roof, with the same team.
