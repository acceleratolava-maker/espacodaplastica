02

Philosophy of practice

Studying is part of the surgery.

For Dr. Rodrigo, the surgical act is only the visible part of a process that begins long before — in study, in planning, and in an honest conversation about what is possible for each case.

“There is no transformation without learning and knowledge.”
Motto of the Anache Anbar brothers

It is the standard he applies first to himself: whoever teaches other physicians cannot stop learning. In practice, that means consultations conducted calmly — the anatomy, the alternatives, and the limits of each technique, explained without haste — and an individualized plan before any surgical decision.

04

A hospital of their own

Why Dr. Rodrigo operates under his own roof.

He operates in the facility he helped conceive: an in-house surgical center, an internal sterilization department, and recovery with hotel-style amenities — in a hospital where only planned elective surgeries take place, with no emergency room and no admissions for infectious diseases.

For the surgeon, that means control over every stage of the process. For the patient, it means being cared for, from preparation to discharge, by the same team — under the same roof.
